Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bean Bryant (August 23, 1978 â€“ January 26, 2020) was an American professional basketball player. He played his entire 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association (NBA). Bryant was widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of the sport, winning five NBA championships and being named an 18-time All-Star, a 15-time member of the All-NBA Team, and a 12-time member of the All-Defensive Team. He was the 2008 NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP) and a two-time NBA Finals MVP. Bryant also led the NBA in scoring twice and ranks fourth in league all-time regular season and postseason scoring. He was posthumously voted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2020 and named to the NBA 75th Anniversary Team in 2021.
